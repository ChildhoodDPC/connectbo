Insight
  • Overall Trend

    • In general, Crime rate does not show decreasing or increasing pattern in the last 5 years. It maintains a stable pattern from 2015 to 2019.

  • Seasonal Trend

    • Other than this, we found that the crime rate in Chapel Hill would reach its peak during the summer time and its bottom during the winter time.

  • Specific Area

    • Cluster 4(Franklin Street and neighborhood) is a relatively small area with the highest crime rate, almost the sum of adjacent areas. Police should assign more resource in this area rather than spreading too wide.

  • Crime Segmentation
    • Sexual asssult was spotted around Rosemary Street and Franklin Street
    • Traffic accident was most frequently happening at intercection of West Franklin Street and Raleigh Road
    • Animal abuse was found around Rosemary Street and Graham Street
  • Future Research Plan

    • We hope to gather more data, such as time of occurence (night, morning or noon), to further develop our model to give more concrete recommendations for police resource allocation and protect the future of North Carolina.
